Previously I had defined a statistical model as a generative model for the data. In my definition a mathematical model could be made into a statistical model by adding randomness that allows for the data that are actually observed. For example, a mathematical model could be y=a+bx where y and x are known and a and b are parameters to be estimated. A statistical version (regression) is y=a+bx+e where e is now given a probability distribution, e.g. normal with mean zero and unknown variance. One inferential advantage to the latter is that we can estimate a and b and provide uncertainty about their estimates. Many people do not worry about this step and just estimate the parameters and treat them as the truth. This will provide overconfidence in any predictions that are being made.
